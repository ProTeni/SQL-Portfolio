# SQL Portfolio — Business Analysis Across Four Domains

**A collection of SQL projects answering real business questions across DVD rental, brewery sales,
Starbucks customer analytics, and facility management — using joins, CTEs, subqueries, aggregations,
and conditional logic.**

> Each project takes a business brief and answers it in SQL: from "who are our top 5 customers by
> spend?" to "which sales rep deserves the annual promotion?" — written in PostgreSQL.

---

## Why this repo
SQL is the backbone of most data roles. This portfolio shows I can translate business questions
into correct, readable queries across different schemas and domains — not just textbook syntax.

## Projects

### 🎬 DVD Rentals
Optimising a rental store's operations: above-average earning titles, staff performance, MPAA-rating
cost analysis, customer spend tiers, and platinum-loyalty eligibility.
**Techniques:** INNER JOINs, CTEs, correlated subqueries, `CASE` categorisation, `EXTRACT`,
`GROUP BY`/`HAVING`, `LIMIT`.

### 🍺 International Breweries
Helping a brewery find its top sales reps, most loyal customers, and best products — including
quarterly profit analysis, discount calculations, and restock targeting.
**Techniques:** `DISTINCT`, `UPPER`, multi-column `ORDER BY`, filtering with `IN`, `GROUP BY`/`HAVING`,
`FETCH FIRST`.

### 🏢 Facility Management
Querying a leisure-facility database: member fees, maintenance-cost ratios, booking volumes, and
member activity.
**Techniques:** JOINs, `LIKE`/`ILIKE` pattern matching, `IN`, date filtering, `SUM`/`COUNT` with
`GROUP BY`/`HAVING`.

## Tools
PostgreSQL · SQL (joins, CTEs, subqueries, window/conditional logic, schema design)
